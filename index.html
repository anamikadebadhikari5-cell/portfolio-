My Poems

This is Not About the Tin

I see it through the window—
The burning tin in the blazing sun.
I wonder how it stands there,
Enduring the fierce heat.

I see beneath the tin roof,
People sitting quietly.
They sit in peace and comfort,
Under the shade it offers.

Do they ever wonder—
About the tin? Ever?
It burns in the sun
Morning, afternoon, and evening.

Never does it think of itself,
Not once, not ever.
Beneath its shadow live
Birds and people alike.

It bears more than sunlight,
It endures everything—
Storms, rains, and tempests,
Giving shelter to all.

Yet no one thinks of it,
Everyone just thinks of themselves.

You think this is just a tin?
No—
This is not about the tin.

— Anamika Deb Adhikary

Love — Beyond Everything?

If love is pure,
Then it is deep.

If love is truth,
I belong to the truth.

If love is the way,
Then I choose the path to the temple.

If love has meaning,
Then I convey the meaning of the eternal.

If love is peace,
Then let peace shower
      Upon us.

At times, I doubt
If even God knows the true meaning of love.
And if love is a lie,
Would I still choose to be a liar?

— Anamika Deb Adhikary
